# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis — Power BI Dashboard

## Overview

This project analyses telecom customer data to identify the key drivers of churn — understanding *why* customers leave and *which* segments are at highest risk. The goal is to surface actionable insights that a product or business team can use to improve retention.

---

## Business Problem

Customer churn is one of the most critical metrics for any subscription-based or service business. Acquiring a new customer costs significantly more than retaining an existing one. This analysis answers:

- What is the overall churn rate?
- Which customer segments churn the most?
- What are the strongest predictors of churn?
- What retention strategies does the data suggest?

## Project Workflow

**1. Exploratory Analysis**
- Loaded and profiled the dataset in Power Query
- Identified data types, null values, and distribution of key variables
- Created calculated columns and base measures in DAX

**2. Investigating Churn Patterns**
- Analysed churn rate by contract type, tenure, payment method, and service usage
- Identified which customer segments had disproportionately high churn
- Built comparison visuals to surface meaningful differences between churned and retained customers

**3. Visualising the Analysis**
- Built an interactive Power BI dashboard with slicers for dynamic filtering
- Designed KPI cards for overall churn rate, total customers, and churned customers
- Created segment-level breakdowns to support business decision-making

---

## Key Findings

- Customers on **month-to-month contracts** churn at a significantly higher rate than those on annual or two-year contracts
- **New customers (0–12 months tenure)** are the highest-risk group — churn drops sharply after the first year
- Customers without **tech support or online security add-ons** show higher churn rates
- **Electronic check payment** users churn more than those using automatic payment methods

---

## Business Recommendation

Focus retention efforts on:
1. **Incentivising contract upgrades** — offer discounts for customers switching from month-to-month to annual plans
2. **Early tenure engagement** — introduce onboarding programmes for customers in their first 6 months
3. **Bundling value-add services** — proactively offer tech support and security add-ons to at-risk segments
4. **Payment method nudges** — encourage auto-payment setup at sign-up to reduce friction and churn signals
